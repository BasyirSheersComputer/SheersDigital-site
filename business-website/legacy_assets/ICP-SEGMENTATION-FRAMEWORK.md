# WasteWise ICP Segmentation Framework
## Malaysian F&B Market Segmentation by Pricing Tier

**Created**: November 4, 2025  
**Purpose**: Define Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs) aligned with WasteWise pricing tiers  
**Market**: Malaysian F&B establishments

---

## Overview

This framework segments Malaysian F&B establishments into three distinct ICPs, each aligned with WasteWise's pricing tiers and dream outcomes. Each ICP is defined by business characteristics, pain points, and expected outcomes.

---

## ICP TIER 1: QUICK WIN (Entry Level)
### Pricing: RM 2,997/month

### Business Profile
**Business Type:**
- Single-location cafes and restaurants
- Independent food stalls upgrading to restaurants
- Cloud kitchens and ghost kitchens
- Small chains (2-3 outlets max)

**Business Metrics:**
- **Monthly Revenue**: RM 50,000 - RM 200,000
- **Number of Outlets**: 1-3
- **Staff Size**: 10-30 employees
- **Food Cost %**: 35-45% (high, needs optimization)
- **Waste Level**: 10-15% (untracked, significant)
- **Current Tech**: Minimal (Excel, WhatsApp, manual)

**Geographic Focus:**
- Urban areas: KL, Selangor, Penang, JB
- Suburban areas with growth potential
- Food court operators

### Pain Points (Ranked by Priority)
1. **Bleeding cash on food waste** - don't know where it's going
2. **Inventory chaos** - constant stockouts or overstocking
3. **No visibility** - operating blind, no data
4. **Staff issues** - theft, poor portion control
5. **Profit squeeze** - rising costs, can't raise prices

### Dream Outcome
**Primary Goal**: "Stop losing RM 15,000-25,000 monthly to preventable waste within 60 days"

**Success Metrics:**
- 20-30% waste reduction in 60 days
- RM 15-25k monthly savings
- Break-even on investment in 1-2 months
- Simple system that staff actually use

**Psychological Outcome:**
- Feel in control of their business
- Sleep better knowing where money goes
- Confidence to expand

### Decision-Making Profile
- **Decision Maker**: Owner/Founder (hands-on)
- **Decision Speed**: Fast (2-4 weeks)
- **Budget Authority**: Direct control
- **Key Concern**: "Will this actually work for my business?"
- **Proof Needed**: Quick wins, simple ROI, testimonials from similar businesses

### Best-Fit Indicators
✅ Owner actively involved in daily operations  
✅ Currently using Excel/manual tracking  
✅ Experiencing visible waste issues  
✅ Growth-minded (wants to expand)  
✅ Open to technology adoption  
✅ Located in urban/suburban areas  

### Red Flags (Poor Fit)
❌ Not tracking any financial data  
❌ Resistant to change/technology  
❌ Business in decline/survival mode  
❌ No growth ambitions  
❌ Unwilling to invest in staff training

## ICP TIER 2: GROWTH SYSTEM (Preferred - Most Popular)
### Pricing: RM 5,997/month

### Business Profile
**Business Type:**
- Multi-location restaurant chains
- Established café chains (3-10 outlets)
- Hotel F&B operations
- Mid-sized food courts
- Catering companies with volume

**Business Metrics:**
- **Monthly Revenue**: RM 200,000 - RM 1,000,000
- **Number of Outlets**: 3-10 locations
- **Staff Size**: 30-100 employees
- **Food Cost %**: 32-40%
- **Waste Level**: 8-12% (partially tracked)
- **Current Tech**: Mix (POS, basic inventory software)

**Geographic Focus:**
- Multiple locations across Malaysia
- Key cities: KL, Penang, JB, Ipoh, Kuching
- Expansion phase businesses

### Pain Points (Ranked by Priority)
1. **Inconsistency across locations** - each outlet different performance
2. **Scaling challenges** - can't replicate success across outlets
3. **Data fragmentation** - each location uses different systems
4. **Management bandwidth** - can't be everywhere at once
5. **Margin pressure** - growth not translating to profit

### Dream Outcome
**Primary Goal**: "Scale profitably with 35-45% waste reduction and RM 35-50k monthly savings across all locations"

**Success Metrics:**
- 35-45% waste reduction within 90 days
- RM 35-50k monthly savings
- Standardized operations across all outlets
- Data-driven decision making
- 10-15% profit margin increase

**Psychological Outcome:**
- Confidence to expand aggressively
- Central visibility without micromanaging
- Competitive advantage through efficiency
- Professional, scalable operations

### Decision-Making Profile
- **Decision Maker**: Owner + Ops Manager (collaborative)
- **Decision Speed**: Moderate (4-8 weeks)
- **Budget Authority**: Owner approval needed
- **Key Concern**: "Can this handle multiple locations and scale with us?"
- **Proof Needed**: Case studies from similar chains, ROI projections, demo

### Best-Fit Indicators
✅ 3+ locations already operating  
✅ Plans to expand further  
✅ Existing POS and some tech infrastructure  
✅ Dedicated operations team  
✅ Experiencing scaling pains  
✅ Revenue >RM 200k/month  
✅ Invested in brand building  

### Red Flags (Poor Fit)
❌ Single location pretending to scale  
❌ No operations infrastructure  
❌ Owner not ready to delegate  
❌ Poor financial health  
❌ No standardized processes

## ICP TIER 3: ENTERPRISE (Premium)
### Pricing: Custom (Starting RM 15,000+/month)

### Business Profile
**Business Type:**
- Large restaurant chains (10+ outlets)
- National/regional franchises
- Hotel chains with multiple F&B outlets
- Corporate catering operations
- Food manufacturers with retail outlets

**Business Metrics:**
- **Monthly Revenue**: RM 1,000,000+
- **Number of Outlets**: 10-100+ locations
- **Staff Size**: 100-1,000+ employees
- **Food Cost %**: 28-35%
- **Waste Level**: 5-10% (tracked but not optimized)
- **Current Tech**: Enterprise systems (ERP, POS, inventory management)

**Geographic Focus:**
- National presence across Malaysia
- Regional expansion (ASEAN)
- Multiple brands/concepts

### Pain Points (Ranked by Priority)
1. **Enterprise-scale inefficiency** - small % waste = huge RM losses
2. **System integration** - multiple systems don't talk to each other
3. **Compliance at scale** - regulatory burden across locations
4. **Competitive pressure** - need every advantage to stay ahead
5. **Operational excellence** - pursuing world-class standards

### Dream Outcome
**Primary Goal**: "Transform operations with 40-50% waste reduction, RM 100-300k+ monthly savings, and 8% margin increase to dominate the market"

**Success Metrics:**
- 40-50% waste reduction within 6 months
- RM 100-300k+ monthly savings
- 8% profit margin increase
- Full integration with existing enterprise systems
- Competitive advantage through data insights
- Franchise-ready standardization

**Psychological Outcome:**
- Industry leadership position
- Board/investor confidence
- Franchise expansion capability
- Benchmark for competitors
- Sustainable growth platform

### Decision-Making Profile
- **Decision Maker**: C-Suite (CEO, COO, CFO) + Board
- **Decision Speed**: Slow (2-6 months)
- **Budget Authority**: Board approval, formal procurement
- **Key Concern**: "How does this integrate with our enterprise systems and strategic goals?"
- **Proof Needed**: Enterprise case studies, pilot program, vendor stability, security audits

### Best-Fit Indicators
✅ 10+ locations operational  
✅ Enterprise systems in place (ERP/POS)  
✅ Dedicated IT and operations teams  
✅ Strategic growth plans (IPO, franchise expansion)  
✅ Brand protection concerns  
✅ Budget for enterprise software  
✅ Compliance requirements (public company, franchise)  

### Red Flags (Poor Fit)
❌ Complex politics preventing decision  
❌ "Tire kicker" - no real budget  
❌ Unrealistic timeline expectations  
❌ Won't commit to pilot/proof of concept  
❌ Legacy system lock-in with no flexibility

## ICP IDENTIFICATION CRITERIA

### Quick Assessment Questions
Use these to quickly identify ICP tier:

1. **"How many outlets do you operate?"**
   - 1-3 → Tier 1
   - 3-10 → Tier 2
   - 10+ → Tier 3

2. **"What's your approximate monthly revenue?"**
   - RM 50-200k → Tier 1
   - RM 200k-1M → Tier 2
   - RM 1M+ → Tier 3

3. **"What systems do you currently use?"**
   - Excel/Manual → Tier 1
   - POS + basic software → Tier 2
   - Enterprise ERP/Systems → Tier 3

4. **"Who makes technology purchasing decisions?"**
   - Owner directly → Tier 1
   - Owner + Ops Manager → Tier 2
   - C-Suite + Board → Tier 3

5. **"What's your biggest pain point with food waste?"**
   - "Don't know where it's going" → Tier 1
   - "Can't standardize across locations" → Tier 2
   - "Need enterprise integration" → Tier 3

## LEAD QUALIFICATION SCORING

### Scoring System (0-100 points)

**Business Size (25 points)**
- Revenue in target range: 15 points
- Outlet count in target range: 10 points

**Pain Point Match (25 points)**
- Experiencing primary pain points: 15 points
- Actively seeking solutions: 10 points

**Decision-Making (25 points)**
- Access to decision maker: 15 points
- Budget authority confirmed: 10 points

**Best-Fit Indicators (25 points)**
- 5+ best-fit indicators present: 25 points
- 3-4 indicators: 15 points
- 1-2 indicators: 5 points

**Lead Quality Tiers:**
- **90-100 points**: HOT - Priority outreach, direct owner contact
- **75-89 points**: WARM - Strong fit, standard sales process
- **60-74 points**: QUALIFIED - Good fit, nurture sequence
- **Below 60**: COLD - Low priority or nurture long-term

## LEAD SEGMENTATION WORKFLOW

### Step 1: Data Collection
From directories, collect:
- Company name
- Number of locations (if listed)
- Contact phone
- Contact email
- Location/address
- Business type (restaurant, café, chain, etc.)
- Any revenue indicators

### Step 2: Initial Categorization
Based on available data, assign preliminary ICP tier:
- **Tier 1**: Single location or small chain indicators
- **Tier 2**: Multi-location mentions, "chain" in name
- **Tier 3**: "Group", "Holdings", franchise indicators

### Step 3: Qualification Research
For high-value prospects, research:
- Website analysis
- Social media presence
- News mentions
- Job postings (indicates growth)
- Online reviews (volume indicates size)

### Step 4: Lead Scoring
Apply scoring system and prioritize:
1. HOT leads (90-100) → Immediate outreach
2. WARM leads (75-89) → This week outreach
3. QUALIFIED leads (60-74) → This month outreach
4. COLD leads (<60) → Nurture campaign
